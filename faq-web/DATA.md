<summary>1.2 What is VINS? <a class="anchor" href="#q-1-2" aria-label="Link to this question">§</a></summary>
<p><strong>VINS</strong> is the Vicharanashala Internship — an online programme open to anyone who clears our interview. The work is real open-source contribution under a mentor, the certificate is from the Vicharanashala Lab for Education Design at IIT Ropar, and the programme itself is free (we charge nothing). There is no stipend.</p>
<p>If you are seeing a yellow VINS panel on your result page, you are selected.</p>
</details>

1.3 what are the phases of vins, and what do the badges mean? §
vins is structured as four phases. each one is marked by a badge — a small token of where you are in the journey.
🥉 bronze (phase 1) — a short training period at the start, planned
  around what you already know. if you arrive already comfortable with the   basics, your mentor may skip bronze and put you straight on to the project.
🥈 silver (phase 2) — the main work. you contribute to a real
  open-source project under a vicharanashala mentor. finishing bronze and   silver completes your internship and earns the certificate.
🥇 gold (phase 3) — a recognition awarded during silver if your
  contribution stands on its own as a meaningful feature, not just a small   fix.
🏆 platinum (phase 4) — a standing invitation to come back and visit
  the lab — a short trip — any time during the year after your internship   ends. we help with travel through a small visit stipend.
most interns finish at bronze + silver, and that is exactly what the certificate is for. gold and platinum are extras you can pick up if your work makes the case for them. either way, you walk away with a real open-source contribution to your name and a mentor who knows you well.

<summary>1.3 What are the phases of VINS, and what do the badges mean? <a class="anchor" href="#q-1-3" aria-label="Link to this question">§</a></summary>
<p>VINS is structured as four phases. Each one is marked by a badge — a small token of where you are in the journey.</p>
<ul><li>🥉 <strong>Bronze (Phase 1)</strong> — a short training period at the start, planned</li></ul>
<p>  around what you already know. If you arrive already comfortable with the   basics, your mentor may skip Bronze and put you straight on to the project.</p>
<ul><li>🥈 <strong>Silver (Phase 2)</strong> — the main work. You contribute to a real</li></ul>
<p>  open-source project under a Vicharanashala mentor. Finishing Bronze and   Silver completes your internship and earns the certificate.</p>
<ul><li>🥇 <strong>Gold (Phase 3)</strong> — a recognition awarded during Silver if your</li></ul>
<p>  contribution stands on its own as a meaningful feature, not just a small   fix.</p>
<ul><li>🏆 <strong>Platinum (Phase 4)</strong> — a standing invitation to come back and visit</li></ul>
<p>  the lab — a short trip — any time during the year after your internship   ends. We help with travel through a small visit stipend.</p>
<p>Most interns finish at Bronze + Silver, and that is exactly what the certificate is for. Gold and Platinum are extras you can pick up if your work makes the case for them. Either way, you walk away with a real open-source contribution to your name and a mentor who knows you well.</p>
</details>

candidates who have already graduated and are not currently enrolled in any programme are not eligible for this cycle. if you re-enrol later (higher studies, etc.), you are very welcome to apply again in a future cycle.
">
<summary>1.4 Who is the internship for? Are alumni eligible? <a class="anchor" href="#q-1-4" aria-label="Link to this question">§</a></summary>
<p>The internship is for <strong>currently-enrolled students</strong> at any college or university — undergraduate, postgraduate, or doctoral. The NOC requirement is the practical reflection of this: we ask for institutional consent that you can commit your time to this internship.</p>
<p><strong>Candidates who have already graduated and are not currently enrolled in any programme are not eligible</strong> for this cycle. If you re-enrol later (higher studies, etc.), you are very welcome to apply again in a future cycle.</p>
</details>

">
